Simple Boids

Speed

Controls the speed at which the boids move

Cohesion

Controls how much the boids will bunch together

Alignment

Controls how much the boids will match the direction of its neighbours

Separation

Controls how much the boids will avoid each other

Boid Count

Controls how many boids are in the simulation

Boids is an artificial life program developed by Craig Reynolds in 1986. Meant to mimic the movement of flocking birds, it is a great example of emergent behaviour, where individual agents who only follow simple rules can simulate complex behaviour by interacting with one another. Shown here is a simple simulation with adjustable values to change the behaviour of the "boids".
